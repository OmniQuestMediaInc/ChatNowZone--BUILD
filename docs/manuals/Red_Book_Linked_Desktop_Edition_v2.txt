Foreword
As our platform has grown in complexity, scale, and visibility, it has become clear that policy statements 
alone are not sufficient to ensure operational excellence. What protects an organization under pressure 
is not intention — it is structure. The Red Book Corpus has been developed to provide that structure. 
This corpus is a scenario-based operational playbook designed to guide our teams through high-
friction, ambiguous, or high-stakes situations with consistency and clarity. It translates policy into 
action. It converts values into workflows. It ensures that every incident — whether minor or critical — is 
handled in a manner that is calm, documented, defensible, and aligned with our platform doctrine. The 
Red Book is not designed to eliminate judgment. It is designed to scaffold judgment. Each scenario 
establishes: - Why the issue requires structured attention - What deterministic system actions occur 
immediately - What GPT may and may not do - What human review must confirm - What evidence 
must be preserved - When escalation is required - How communication should be framed The structure 
embedded within these scenarios reflects a core principle: Compliance must be a system property, not 
a policy paragraph. This corpus supports: - Customer service agents - Trust & Safety personnel 
Payments operations - Engineering escalation teams - Executive decision-makers It ensures that when 
we act, we act consistently. When we escalate, we escalate intentionally. When we communicate, we 
do so with clarity and restraint. And when we are audited — internally or externally — we can 
demonstrate what was done, by whom, and when. The Red Book is not reactive. It is preparatory. It is 
intended to reduce ambiguity, protect human wellbeing, preserve financial integrity, and reinforce 
institutional credibility. This is not simply documentation. It is operational discipline.
Agent Training Deck — Operational Use of the Red 
Book
Slide 1 — Purpose of the Red Book
The Red Book provides structured guidance for handling incidents consistently. It is a decision-support 
framework, not a replacement for judgment.
Slide 2 — The Core Doctrine
Deterministic systems execute financial and enforcement actions. GPT drafts and summarizes. 
Humans review and authorize.
Slide 3 — Incident Lifecycle
1. Intake 2. Immediate deterministic containment 3. Documentation and evidence capture 4. Escalation 
(if required) 5. Resolution 6. Post-incident review
Slide 4 — Severity Levels
Severity 1: Localized, reversible. Severity 2: Multi-account or financial impact risk. Severity 3: Material 
trust or compliance risk. Severity 4: Immediate human safety or platform continuity risk.
Slide 5 — Opening Conversations
Always begin calmly. Thank the reporter. Clarify facts. Avoid assigning blame. Avoid promising 
outcomes.
Slide 6 — What GPT Can Do
Draft responses. Summarize logs. Suggest escalation tiers. Identify missing required fields.
Slide 7 — What GPT Cannot Do
Freeze accounts. Issue refunds. Terminate streams. Override policy. Promise outcomes.
Slide 8 — Evidence Standards
Every incident must record: - Who acted - What was done - When it occurred - Why the action was 
taken - Supporting artifacts
Slide 9 — Escalation Discipline
Escalation is mandatory when: - Safety risk exists - Legal exposure is possible - Financial integrity is at 
risk - Media visibility increases risk.
Slide 10 — Communication Discipline
Do not disclose investigative outcomes. Do not speculate. Use approved messaging templates. Log 
every external communication.
Slide 11 — Emergency Protocol
When safety is uncertain: Pause or terminate broadcast. Preserve evidence. Notify Trust & Safety and 
Exec if required.
Slide 12 — Refund & Settlement Handling
Tokens are non-refundable per policy. Above-policy settlements follow structured matrix. Wallet 
snapshot required at intake. Spending freeze during review.
Slide 13 — Fraud & Abuse Signals
Velocity anomalies. Device mismatch. Credential stuffing. Emergency report abuse patterns.
Slide 14 — Media & Public Narrative
Default posture: controlled, neutral. No comment unless approved. Statements only via official 
channels.
Slide 15 — Moderator Wellbeing
Graphic or distress content requires rotation. Debrief required for Severity 4 exposure. Escalate 
emotional fatigue concerns.
Slide 16 — System Integrity
Logs must be immutable. Actions must be attributable. Settlement calculations must be deterministic.
Slide 17 — When in Doubt
Pause. Escalate. Document. Do not improvise outside structure.
Slide 18 — The Objective
Protect users. Protect creators. Protect the platform. Protect operational credibility.

Flagaccount risk score - Prevent settlement duplication

settlementoffer (2/5 token logic, deterministic calculation outside GPT) - Flag inconsistencies for 
human review Human Response Playbook:

settlement. 4. Send structured offer or deny per policy. 5. Log rationale and attach evidence.
